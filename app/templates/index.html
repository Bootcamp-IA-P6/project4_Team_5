About OopsBin

Model

ResNet50 with transfer learning from ImageNet, fine-tuned in 3 progressive phases for waste classification.

ResNet50 is a convolutional neural network with 50 layers, pre-trained on ImageNet (1.2 million images, 1000 classes). We froze the backbone and added a custom classification head: GlobalAveragePooling2D, Dropout (0.3), Dense (256, ReLU), Dropout (0.3), and Dense (7, Softmax). Training was done in 3 phases: Phase 1 trained only the head for 20 epochs (lr=1e-3), Phase 2 fine-tuned the last 30 layers for 15 epochs (lr=1e-5), and Phase 3 continued until EarlyStopping at epoch 4 (lr=5e-6). Total trainable parameters in Phase 1: 526,343.

Dataset

Trained on 16,756 images from 3 public datasets, unified into 7 waste categories.

Three datasets were combined: Garbage Classification (6 classes, 2,527 images), Garbage Classification 12 classes (12 classes, 15,515 images), and RealWaste (9 classes, 4,752 images). Classes were mapped and unified into 7 categories: cardboard, glass, metal, organic, paper, plastic, and trash. Textile was excluded due to low representation across datasets. Glass subclasses (brown, green, white) were merged. The dataset was capped at 2,500 images per class to reduce imbalance and split 80/10/10 into train (11,036), validation (1,376), and test (1,385).

Accuracy

93.3% test accuracy with only 2.6% overfitting. Precision, recall, and F1 above 0.88 for all classes.

The model achieved 95.9% training accuracy and 93.3% test accuracy, giving an overfitting margin of just 2.6% (well below the 5% threshold). Best performing classes: organic (F1=0.97), paper (F1=0.96), cardboard and glass (F1=0.95). Most challenging: trash (F1=0.88) due to visual diversity, and plastic (F1=0.90) due to confusion with metal. EarlyStopping with patience=3 and ReduceLROnPlateau ensured optimal convergence without overfitting.

Architecture

Frozen ResNet50 backbone + custom classification head with dropout regularization and data augmentation.

All images were resized to 224x224 and preprocessed with ResNet50's standard preprocessing (ImageNet mean subtraction, BGR conversion). Data augmentation was applied only to the training set: RandomFlip (horizontal), RandomRotation (0.2), RandomZoom (0.2), RandomBrightness (0.2), and RandomContrast (0.2). The tf.data pipeline with prefetching was used for efficient GPU utilization. Dropout layers (p=0.3) in the classification head provided regularization during training.
